  3. Question

    25 University of Arkansas pre-law students are taking the LSAT exam (the standardized exam for entry into law school) where the average LSAT score in the United States is 173 with a standard devation of 13. To be a top-ranked institution, the average student score must be in the in the top 80% of classes. What average score is needed for the University to be a top ranked school?

    Below is R code for the problem that can be used to solve the problem.

    qnorm(0.99, 0, 1)
    ## 2.326
    qnorm(173, 99, 13)
    ## 1.000
    
qnorm(0.8, 0, 1)

    ## 0.842
    qnorm(80, 173, 13)
    ## 0.000

    Solution

    The Z-score for the average students’ scores are Z=Z = qnorm(0.8) =0.842= 0.842. From the Z-score, we can calculate the LSAT score needed by solving Z=x‾−μσnZ = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu}{\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}} for x‾\bar{x} getting x‾=Z*σn+μ=0.842*13/5+173=175.188\bar{x} = Z * \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} + \mu = 0.842 * 13 / 5 + 173 = 175.188.
